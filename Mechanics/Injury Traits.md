# Injury Traits

When a Hunter takes damage, the type of injury inflicted depends on the **Attack Trait** of the damaging ability or weapon. Different attack traits cause different injury tables to be rolled from.

## Core Attack Traits

### Slashing
**Weapons:** Swords, axes, claws, scythes
**Characteristics:** Deep cuts, bleeding wounds, severed tendons and muscles

Slashing injuries emphasize:
- Bleeding effects (immediate and ongoing)
- Mutilations (loss of function, scarring)
- Muscle and tendon damage

### Piercing
**Weapons:** Daggers, spears, crossbow bolts, arrows, rapiers
**Characteristics:** Deep puncture wounds, organ damage, precise but deadly

Piercing injuries emphasize:
- Minimal surface bleeding but deep internal damage
- Organ hits at serious thresholds
- Crippling wounds that disable specific body parts
- High lethality at serious+ damage

### Blunt
**Weapons:** Staffs, maces, hammers, fists, shields
**Characteristics:** Crushing impact, broken bones, concussions, internal bruising

Blunt injuries emphasize:
- Bone fractures (limbs, ribs, skull)
- Concussions and head trauma
- Bruising that compounds damage
- Internal injuries

### Fire
**Sources:** Flame Burst, Incinerative Smite, Flask of Fire, Alchemical Bomb (Fire)
**Characteristics:** Burns, tissue damage, ongoing burning

Fire injuries emphasize:
- Burns (varying degrees)
- Tissue destruction
- Ongoing burning damage

### Lightning
**Sources:** Arching Lightning
**Characteristics:** Electrical shock, nerve damage, stunning

Lightning injuries emphasize:
- Nerve damage
- Muscle spasms
- Stun effects

### Acid
**Sources:** Alchemical Bomb (Acid)
**Characteristics:** Chemical burns, tissue dissolution

Acid injuries emphasize:
- Chemical burns
- Ongoing tissue damage

### Frost
**Sources:** Alchemical Bomb (Frost)
**Characteristics:** Freezing, tissue damage, numbness

Frost injuries emphasize:
- Frostbite
- Reduced mobility
- Numbness affecting combat ability

## Injury Thresholds

| Applied Damage | Injury Severity |
|----------------|-----------------|
| 1-4 | [[Light injury]] |
| 5-9 | [[Medium Injury]] |
| 10-19 | [[Serious Injury]] |
| 20+ | Death |

When rolling for injuries, roll 1dX (where X is the number of options in that category) to determine the specific injury.

## Notes

- Abilities that specify "Use Weapon Stats" inherit the weapon's attack trait
- Multi-trait attacks (e.g., Fire + Light) use the primary damage trait for injury determination
- Some abilities may specify custom injury effects that override the normal tables
